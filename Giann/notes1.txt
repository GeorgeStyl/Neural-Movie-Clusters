According to the euclidean distance function, Pair B is closer than Pair A. But intuitively, isn't Pair B
showing much stronger evidence of similar taste?
Euclidean distance function is implicitly punishing users for having more common ground because distance accumulates
over dimensions. This creates a counter-intuitive situation where:

    More evidence of similarity → potentially larger distance

    Less evidence → potentially smaller distance


THE FIRST METRIC (dist euclidean), we can say that measures: "average disagreement per common item".
A better way to represent it, would be to divide the quantity the function gives with the number of common items.
